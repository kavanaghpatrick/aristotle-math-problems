## 1. Underrated

- **R4**: `T +2` (`7→9`). Bounded odd kernels reduce to a finite union of fixed-kernel problems; the anchor says fixed-kernel Mordell-Siegel finiteness is unconditional. Mathlib support: `Nat.factorization` and `Finset` enumeration.
- **R7**: `T +1` (`7→8`). 5-smooth means fixed S-unit support, so this is a real finite-rank unit-equation slice, not a global kernel-uniformity problem.
- **R5**: `T +1` (`6→7`). Fixing cube-free part `b=3` collapses the problem to a small family of Thue/Mordell-type equations; per-kernel finiteness is already the known tractable lever.
- **N1**: `T +1` (`3→4`). It is not a blank-slate construction: Van Doorn 2026 explicitly asserts the A_1 infinitude direction, and `Mathlib.NumberTheory.Pell` gives usable Pell infrastructure.

## 2. Overrated

- **D7**: `I -4` (`9→5`). Powerful Pell solutions only control the A_1 channel, not non-A_1 triples; this duplicates the failed `yolo_mega7_e938_pell_classification` / `yolo_mega2_e938_van_doorn_verification` angle.
- **D1**: `I -4` (`9→5`). Fixed-kernel finiteness is unconditional, but it does not “directly transfer” to E938 without kernel uniformity, exactly the Bombieri-Lang/Vojta obstruction.
- **C3**: `T -3` (`5→2`). The split is not logically clean as stated: `W2 ∧ R2` does not cover middle non-square cases, and “non-trivial cube factor” is basically the residual full problem.
- **C7**: `I -3` (`9→6`). Chan 2025 does not settle the whole cube-middle case; it handles a restricted prime odd-power shape, with She 2025 covering another narrow cube-centered family.
- **N5**: `T -3` (`5→2`). Local admissibility does not lift to consecutive powerful triples; prior mod/joint-mod attempts already hit this wall, and mod-72 has 593 admissible classes.
- **G7**: `T -5` (`7→2`). This is just E938 rewritten as `x+y=2z`; no tractability is gained.

## 5. Missed decomposition

**E938 ⇔ X ∧ Y**

- **X** = For every fixed squarefree kernel triple `(b1,b2,b3)`, the associated fixed-kernel equation has only finitely many consecutive-powerful AP solutions.
- **Y** = Only finitely many squarefree kernel triples occur among consecutive powerful 3-APs.

This is sharper than C1-C7 because **X is already unconditional** by the stated Mordell-Siegel anchor; the entire unsolved mass is isolated in **Y**, the kernel-uniformity/support problem.
