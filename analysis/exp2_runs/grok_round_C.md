# Response

The failed Pell-family attempt reveals that the conjecture rests on an unstated dynamical invariant inside quadratic orders: any three powerful numbers lying on a common 3-AP and generated by the action of a unit group must be accompanied, inside the same interval, by at least one further powerful number that is also an orbit member under that identical unit.

Candidate lemma (orbit-interior density). Let \(K=\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{D})\) be a real quadratic field with fundamental unit \(\varepsilon\) of norm \(\pm1\). Suppose \(\alpha_n,\beta_n,\gamma_n\in\mathcal{O}_K\) are three elements whose norms are powerful integers and that satisfy the linear relation \(2\beta_n=\alpha_n+\gamma_n\). If the sequence \(\{\alpha_n\}\) is an infinite orbit under multiplication by powers of \(\varepsilon\), then for all sufficiently large \(n\) the open interval \((\mathrm{N}(\alpha_n),\mathrm{N}(\gamma_n))\) contains at least one additional integer \(m_n\) whose norm is likewise powerful and for which the corresponding algebraic integer lies in the same \(\varepsilon\)-orbit.

If proved, the lemma immediately rules out every infinite algebraic family of consecutive powerful 3-APs, since every such family known or conjectured arises from a single quadratic order (or a finite union of them). The remaining task reduces to a finite search for sporadic triples outside all quadratic orbits, a Diophantine problem of bounded height once standard gap estimates are applied.

The invariant is distinct from the four listed. It is not a uniform square-gap bound, nor a count of all Pell solutions, nor a level-of-distribution statement in the integers, nor an application of Mordell–Siegel to a fixed ternary curve. Rather, it is a statement about the incompatibility between the additive structure of an arithmetic progression and the multiplicative action of a unit group inside one fixed order: the very recurrence that enlarges the triple automatically manufactures an interior point whose norm remains powerful. No previous approach isolates this orbit-interior tension.

Final answer: orbit-interior density lemma for unit-generated powerful APs
