1.  Lemma A: `consecutive_powerful_ap_reduces_to_coprime_squarefree_d`
    ```lean
    theorem consecutive_powerful_ap_reduces_to_coprime_squarefree_d (k : ℕ) :
        let n0 := Nat.nth Nat.Powerful k
        let n1 := Nat.nth Nat.Powerful (k + 1)
        let n2 := Nat.nth Nat.Powerful (k + 2)
        let d  := n1 - n0
        n1 - n0 = n2 - n1 →
        ∃ (m r : ℕ), m.Powerful ∧ r.Squarefree ∧ Nat.Coprime m r ∧
        Nat.Powerful (m + r) ∧ Nat.Powerful (m + 2 * r) ∧
        n0 = Nat.gcd n0 d * m ∧ d = Nat.gcd n0 d * r := by sorry
    ```
    Proof sketch: Let `G = Nat.gcd n0 d`. By slot 1343, `G` is powerful. Define `m = n0 / G` and `r = d / G`. Then `Nat.Coprime m r`. The AP is `G·m, G·(m+r), G·(m+2r)`. We must show `m`, `m+r`, `m+2r` are powerful and `r` is squarefree. The powerful property for `m, m+r, m+2r` derives from `n0, n1, n2` being powerful, `G` being powerful, and `gcd(G, m)=1, gcd(G, m+r)=1, gcd(G, m+2r)=1` (this is the core structural check). The squarefree nature of `r` follows from `p|d, p^2 ∤ d => p ∤ n` (slot_1329), which implies that if `r` has a square factor, then `d` would have a `p^2` factor, and `G` would also have to "absorb" prime factors to ensure `m` is powerful in the coprime setting.
