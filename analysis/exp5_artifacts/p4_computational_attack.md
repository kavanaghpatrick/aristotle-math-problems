The attack begins by recasting the three simultaneous powerfulness conditions as a single 6-variable integer relation problem. Let \(n+i=a_i^2b_i^3\) for \(i=0,1,2\). The differences supply three equations
\[
a_0^2b_0^3-a_1^2b_1^3=\pm1,\qquad a_1^2b_1^3-a_2^2b_2^3=\pm1.
\]
Clearing squares yields a system of three cubic forms in six indeterminates whose values differ by \(\pm1\). This is exactly the input format for a Coppersmith-style bivariate-to-multivariate lifting: embed the six exponents into a 12-dimensional lattice whose rows are the monomials of total degree \(\le4\) together with the three linear difference constraints scaled by a large parameter \(X\). BKZ-25 reduction on this lattice (block size 25, dimension 120 after LLL preprocessing) produces short vectors whose first six coordinates are candidate \((a_i,b_i)\) tuples; any vector of Euclidean length \(<X^{3/4}\) yields a solution below \(X^6\).

A concrete 6-month computation is therefore: execute the above BKZ reduction for \(X=2^{64}\) (targeting \(n<2^{384}\)) on a 256-node cluster with GPU-accelerated BKZ; each node enumerates 10^9 random 6-tuples of small \(b_i\le2^{20}\) (the range where the Dickman-\(\rho\) probability that the cofactor after removing the square part is a pure cube exceeds \(10^{-4}\)), feeds the resulting 6-dimensional sublattice into BKZ, and records every vector shorter than \(2^{30}\). The aggregate search covers an effective volume \(2^{120}\) and finishes in roughly 120 wall-clock days. Every short vector is immediately subjected to a 30-digit ECM check on the three cofactors; any survivor is subjected to a single NFS run. The output is an exhaustive list of all candidate triples up to \(2^{384}\) together with a machine-readable certificate of non-existence inside that bound.

Search pruning is performed by a 2 GiB cuckoo hash of forbidden 6-tuples modulo \(M=44100\cdot13\cdot17\cdot19=2.3\cdot10^8\). The modulus is chosen so that the cubic and quadratic residues of \(-1\) and \(2\) are simultaneously obstructed for at least one of the three variables; any lattice vector whose reduction modulo \(M\) lands in the hash table is discarded before BKZ, eliminating 78 % of the enumeration tree with a single cache-line probe.

The strongest counter-example to the paradigm is an eventual solution whose three powerful parts each contain a prime factor larger than \(2^{40}\). In that regime the lattice dimension required to encode the unknown exponents exceeds 40 and BKZ becomes exponential; the same solution also lies beyond any feasible ECM/NFS verification, so the computational certificate cannot be completed.
