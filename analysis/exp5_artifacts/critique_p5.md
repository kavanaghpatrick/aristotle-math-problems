**Attack A critique**  
The additive framing via CLP on \(P_X^3\) and Bohr-set increments never sees that powerful numbers are images of integral points on the weighted projective surface \(a^2b^3=x\), so the polynomial factorisation cannot detect the height growth forced by Vojta.  
Embed the three consecutive conditions into the integral-point problem on the surface \(a_0^2b_0^3-a_1^2b_1^3=1\), \(a_1^2b_1^3-a_2^2b_2^3=1\) inside a suitable moduli space of elliptic curves and apply Faltings directly to the resulting cover of high genus.

**Attack B critique**  
The Dirichlet-series and Heath-Brown decomposition treat the sextic-full condition as an L-function question, yet never produce a global section or height inequality on the actual variety cut out by the three simultaneous power conditions, so the claimed pole obstruction remains formal.  
Replace the hybrid sieve by the Vojta inequality on the threefold obtained by blowing up the diagonal in the product of three copies of the weighted surface \(a^2b^3=x\), using the canonical class plus the three shift divisors to bound the height and force only finitely many integral points.  
HIGH  

**Attack C critique**  
Major/minor-arc analysis and Vinogradov mean-value estimates operate on generating functions rather than on the arithmetic surface whose integral points encode the triple, so the vanishing of the singular series modulo 44100 is merely local and supplies no height bound.  
Compactify the equation \(x(x+1)(x+2)=y^2z^3\) to a smooth model over \(\mathbb{Z}\) and apply the Vojta conjecture (or known cases via moduli of elliptic curves with level structure) to the resulting log-general-type pair, yielding an explicit height bound that kills all sufficiently large solutions.  
HIGH  

**Attack D critique**  
Lattice-reduction and Coppersmith searches are finite-precision approximations that cannot detect the global geometric obstruction coming from the canonical class on the surface of consecutive powerful numbers; they therefore remain blind to any solution larger than the running bound.  
Run a Chabauty–Coleman computation on the étale covers of the curve obtained by fixing two of the radical parameters and varying the third, using p-adic heights to prove there are no integral points beyond the range already checked by machine.  
MED  

Attack C is the only one whose contour-shifting language can be reinterpreted as a height estimate on an auxiliary cover.
