## Lemma A (single-base residue subgroup). For every modulus M ‚Č• 1, every d, k:
> `(d^k¬∑B_d) mod M  =  gcd(d^k, M) ¬∑ (‚Ą§/M)`   ‚ÄĒ the subgroup of index gcd(d^k,M) wait, of
> ORDER M/gcd(d^k,M), i.e. all multiples of gcd(d^k,M) in ‚Ą§/M.

**Proof.** `d^k¬∑B_d` is the set of finite sums of distinct elements of P := {d^j : j ‚Č• k}.
Mod M this is the submonoid of (‚Ą§/M,+) generated by {d^j mod M : j‚Č•k}. A finite additively-closed
submonoid of a finite abelian group containing 0 is a subgroup. Its generator is
gcd over ‚Ą§ of {d^j : j‚Č•k} reduced mod M, and gcd_{j‚Č•k}(d^j) = d^k, so the subgroup is
gcd(d^k,M)¬∑(‚Ą§/M). (Repeated power-residues let us add the same value many times, giving the full
subgroup, not just the subset-sum ‚ÄĒ verified for d‚ąą{3,4,5,6,7,9,15}, k‚Č§3, M‚ąą{12,84,100,77,210,343}.) ‚ąé

## Theorem B (sumset residue coverage). For every modulus M ‚Č• 1:
> `T(D,k) mod M  =  gcd( gcd_{d‚ąąD}(d^k), M ) ¬∑ (‚Ą§/M)`.
>
> In particular: **`T(D,k)` meets every residue class mod M  ‚üļ  gcd_{d‚ąąD}(d^k) is coprime to M**,
> and **`T(D,k)` covers ‚Ą§/M for ALL M  ‚üļ  gcd_{d‚ąąD}(d^k) = 1  ‚üļ  gcd(D) = 1.**

**Proof.** The sumset mod M is the subgroup sum ‚ąĎ_d [gcd(d^k,M)¬∑(‚Ą§/M)] (Lemma A), which is the
subgroup generated by {gcd(d^k,M) : d‚ąąD}, i.e. gcd_{d‚ąąD} gcd(d^k,M)¬∑(‚Ą§/M) =
gcd(gcd_{d‚ąąD}(d^k), M)¬∑(‚Ą§/M). The "for all M" statement: gcd_D(d^k)=1 ‚üļ no prime divides every d^k
‚üļ no prime divides every d ‚üļ gcd(D)=1. ‚ąé  (Verified: every admissible gcd=1 family tested covers
‚Ą§/M for all M<200, k‚Č§2; every gcd>1 family is trapped in p¬∑‚Ą§.)

## Corollaries (the payoff)

1. **gcd(D)=1 is exactly the no-congruential-obstruction condition.** This is *why* BEGL96 must add
   gcd(D)=1 for k‚Č•1 and not for k=0: at k=0, d^0=1 so gcd_D(d^0)=1 automatically. The gcd hypothesis
   is not ad hoc ‚ÄĒ it is forced by, and exactly cancels, the mod-d^k obstruction. (Cross-ref:
   sumset_reduction_scaling.md proves gcd>1 ‚üĻ NOT cofinite; Theorem B proves gcd=1 ‚üĻ no congruential
   obstruction survives.)

2. **The problem reduces to a purely archimedean/gap question.** With gcd(D)=1 there is NO modular
   obstruction at any scale. So if cofiniteness FAILS for some admissible D, it fails for a "gap/
   density" reason (some large interval has too few representations), NOT a congruence reason. This
   tells the falsification team (maverick/breaker) where to look: large *interval* gaps, not
   *arithmetic progressions* that are entirely missed (those can't exist when gcd=1).

3. **It makes the BEGL96 interval-covering proof plausibly liftable.** The k=0 proof (per Grok's
   summary of BEGL96) is: self-similarity `B_d = (B_d‚ą©[0,d^m)) + d^m¬∑B_d` + the residue condition
   `‚ąĎ1/(d‚ąí1)‚Č•1` ‚üĻ admissible residues tile ‚Ą§/lcm(d‚ąí1) ‚üĻ a seed interval propagates. For k‚Č•1,
   Theorem B supplies the residue-tiling step (now mod lcm of the relevant moduli, with gcd=1
   guaranteeing completeness). What remains is the SEED: show a single sufficiently long interval is
   fully covered. The seed length grows with k (empirically threshold ‚ąľ d_max^k, see
   sumset_empirical_landscape.md), which is exactly the residual difficulty.

## What is NOT yet proved (honest boundary)

Theorem B kills the congruence obstruction but does NOT prove cofiniteness. Covering every residue
class mod every M is necessary but not sufficient: you also need the representations to be dense
enough that no long interval is skipped. The remaining open content of (Q) is precisely:

> Given gcd(D)=1 (no congruence obstruction) and ‚ąĎ1/(d‚ąí1)‚Č•1 (density ‚Č• critical), does a fully-
> covered seed interval of length ‚Č• (max scale) exist, so self-similarity propagates?

This is the "seed interval" lemma ‚ÄĒ the heart that BEGL96 verified by finite computation for (3,4,7)
and that must be made uniform in D and k for the general theorem. See sumset_seed_interval.md (next).
