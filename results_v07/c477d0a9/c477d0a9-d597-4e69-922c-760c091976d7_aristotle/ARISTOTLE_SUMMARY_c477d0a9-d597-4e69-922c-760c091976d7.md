# Summary of changes
## Erdős Problem 850 — Status: OPEN (sorry remains)

The theorem `erdos_850_no_triple` in `RequestProject/Erdos850.lean` remains unproven with `sorry`. This is expected — **Erdős Problem 850 is a genuinely open conjecture** in number theory with no known proof in the mathematical literature.

### What was attempted

Three substantive proof strategies were tried with the theorem proving subagent:

1. **Direct divisibility approach**: Writing y = a·rad(x), etc., and using coprimality of consecutive integers to constrain the multipliers. Failed because establishing size bounds on the multipliers is essentially equivalent to the conjecture itself.

2. **Radical product bound**: Showing rad(x)·rad(x+1)·rad(x+2) divides the gap d = y−x via pairwise coprimality, then arguing the product exceeds d. The coprimality-based divisibility constraints are valid, but the crucial upper bound on d cannot be established without deeper tools.

3. **Gap constraint analysis**: Exploiting that d = y−x must be simultaneously divisible by rad(x), rad(x+1), and rad(x+2). The pairwise coprimality of consecutive radicals forces rad(x)·rad(x+1) | d and rad(x+1)·rad(x+2) | d, but closing the argument requires a bound like d < rad(x)·rad(x+1)·rad(x+2), which is not provable from the hypotheses alone.

### Why it's hard

The abc conjecture would imply this result (and much stronger statements), but the abc conjecture itself is unproven. Without it, there is no known technique to establish the necessary size bounds on numbers with prescribed radical constraints across three consecutive integers.
