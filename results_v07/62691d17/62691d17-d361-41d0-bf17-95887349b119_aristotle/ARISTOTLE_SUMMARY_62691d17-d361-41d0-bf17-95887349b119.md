### Why the `sorry` remains

**This is a genuinely open problem.** Erdős Problem 267 asks whether `∑ 1/n_k` is irrational whenever `(n_k)` is a strictly increasing sequence of positive integers with `n_{k+1}/n_k → c` for some `1 < c < 2`. No proof or counterexample is known as of 2025.

**What is known:**
- **c ≥ 2 (solved):** Badea's criterion (1993) and related results establish irrationality when the ratio is at least 2, since the sequence grows fast enough to satisfy Oppenheim-type gap conditions.
- **1 < c < 2 (open):** The growth rate is too slow for any known irrationality criterion to apply. The sequence grows geometrically but not fast enough for the quadratic gap condition `n_{k+1} > n_k(n_k − 1) + 1` that underlies most classical results.

The theorem proving subagent was unable to find a proof, consistent with the problem's open status. The file includes detailed documentation of the problem's history, what is known, and why the intermediate regime is difficult.
