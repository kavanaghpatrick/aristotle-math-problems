OPEN GAP: Erdős 124 — Residue Coverage Lemma for the BEGL96 sumset
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/124.lean (erdos124.ne_zero)
Domain: nt

Let B_d = {sums of distinct powers d^j, j ≥ 0} (base-d 0/1-digit numbers), and for
k ≥ 1 let the BEGL96 sumset be Σ(D,k) = ∑_{d∈D} d^k · B_d (pointwise sumset over a
finite set D of integers ≥ 3). The open question: for a finite D with all d ≥ 3,
does Σ(D,k) reduced modulo an arbitrary modulus M cover every residue class of ℤ/M,
for every M and every k ≥ 1, exactly when gcd(D) = 1?

Status: OPEN (component of the open BEGL96 conjecture). gcd(D)=1 is known NECESSARY
(if a prime p divides every d then p divides every summand, so the sumset misses every
residue not ≡ 0 mod p). The forward direction — gcd(D)=1 SUFFICES for full residue
coverage at every M and every k≥1 — is the content. This isolates the congruence
("local") half of BEGL96 from the density half (∑1/(d−1)≥1, which is orthogonal and
not used here).
