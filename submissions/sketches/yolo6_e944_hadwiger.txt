OPEN GAP: Erdős 944 — Dirac's conjecture, k=4 case
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/944.lean
Domain: combinatorics

Dirac (1970) asked: for every k ≥ 4, does there exist a finite simple graph
G with chromatic number k such that every vertex is critical (removing any
vertex strictly drops χ) yet every single edge is non-critical (no
single-edge deletion drops χ)? Several cases at k ≥ 5 are resolved
positively. The case k = 4 remains OPEN.

theorem yolo6_erdos_944_k_eq_four :
    ∃ (V : Type) (_ : Fintype V) (G : SimpleGraph V),
      G.IsCritical 4 ∧
      (∀ e : Sym2 V, ¬ G.IsCriticalEdge e) := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. The k = 4 case evades every existing positive construction.
The bare gap is the existence (or non-existence) of a finite
4-vertex-critical simple graph in which no single edge is critical, using
FormalConjecturesForMathlib's canonical predicates SimpleGraph.IsCritical
and SimpleGraph.IsCriticalEdge from
FormalConjecturesForMathlib/Combinatorics/SimpleGraph/Coloring.lean.
