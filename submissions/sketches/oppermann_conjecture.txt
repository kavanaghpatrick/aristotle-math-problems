OPEN GAP: Oppermann's Conjecture
Source: Oppermann (1882)
Domain: nt

For every integer x ≥ 2, there exists a prime in the interval
(x² - x, x²) and a prime in the interval (x², x² + x).

Implies Legendre's conjecture, Brocard's conjecture, and Andrica's
conjecture. Stronger than all known prime gap results.

Best unconditional: Baker-Harman-Pintz (2001) give a prime in
[x, x + x^{0.525}], far from the x^{0.5} gap Oppermann requires.

theorem oppermann (x : ℕ) (hx : x ≥ 2) :
    (∃ p, Nat.Prime p ∧ x^2 - x < p ∧ p < x^2) ∧
    (∃ p, Nat.Prime p ∧ x^2 < p ∧ p < x^2 + x) := by sorry

Status: OPEN. Implies Legendre and Andrica. No unconditional proof.
