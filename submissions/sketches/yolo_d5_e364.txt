OPEN GAP: Erdős 364 — mod-36 closure for consecutive powerful triples
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/364.lean
Domain: nt

A natural number n is powerful iff for every prime p dividing n, p^2 also
divides n. The conjecture below asserts: if n, n+1, and n+2 are all powerful,
then n is congruent to 7, 27, or 35 modulo 36. This is an unconditional
elementary modular partial result toward the full conjecture (no consecutive
powerful triple), and is not currently formalized in Mathlib.

theorem erdos_364_mod36
    (n : ℕ) (hn : Powerful n) (hn1 : Powerful (n + 1)) (hn2 : Powerful (n + 2)) :
