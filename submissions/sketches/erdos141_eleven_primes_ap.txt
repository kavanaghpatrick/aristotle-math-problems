OPEN GAP: Erdős 141 variant — 11 consecutive primes in arithmetic progression
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/141.lean (erdos_141.variant.eleven)
Domain: number theory / combinatorics

Do there exist 11 consecutive primes p, p+d, p+2d, …, p+10d in arithmetic
progression (i.e. with no prime strictly between consecutive terms)?
The case k ≤ 10 has been verified; the case k = 11 has been open since
the 1960s and remains unresolved.

theorem erdos_141_eleven :
    ∃ (s : Set ℕ), s.IsAPAndPrimeProgressionOfLength 11 := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. The case k = 10 was settled in 1998 by an explicit progression
with common difference 210 = 7#. Any 11-term consecutive prime AP must have
common difference divisible by 11# = 2310. Whether such a tuple exists is
unknown.
