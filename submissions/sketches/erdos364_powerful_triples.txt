OPEN GAP: Erdős Problem 364 — No Three Consecutive Powerful Numbers
Source: Erdős Problem 364
Domain: nt

There do not exist three consecutive positive integers n, n+1, n+2
that are all powerful (every prime divisor appears with exponent ≥ 2).

Golomb proved consecutive powerful pairs exist (e.g., 8, 9).
No consecutive powerful triple has ever been found.

theorem erdos364 : ¬ ∃ n : ℕ, n ≥ 1 ∧
    (∀ p : ℕ, Nat.Prime p → p ∣ n → p ^ 2 ∣ n) ∧
    (∀ p : ℕ, Nat.Prime p → p ∣ (n + 1) → p ^ 2 ∣ (n + 1)) ∧
    (∀ p : ℕ, Nat.Prime p → p ∣ (n + 2) → p ^ 2 ∣ (n + 2)) := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. Pairs exist. No triple found. ABC implies finiteness.
