OPEN GAP: Erdős 942 — limsup of powerful-counts in [n^2,(n+1)^2) is infinite
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/942.lean (variants.limsup); erdosproblems.com/942
Domain: nt

Let h(n) count powerful integers in [n^2, (n+1)^2):
  h(n) = #{m : n^2 ≤ m < (n+1)^2, ∀ prime p | m, p^2 | m}.
Erdős states (and erdosproblems.com confirms) that limsup_{n→∞} h(n) = ∞.
The main conjecture (existence of c > 0 with h(n) ≪ (log n)^{c+o(1)} and
h(n) ≫ (log n)^{c-o(1)} i.o.) is the open problem. The limsup variant is
graduate-tagged in formal-conjectures and proved unconditionally in
De Koninck–Luca (Acta Arith. 114 (2004), 149–157) with quantitative
strengthening h(n) ≫ (log n / log log n)^{1/3} infinitely often.
