OPEN GAP: Bounded-cardinality powerful factorial sums (E1108 sub-claim)
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/1108.lean
Domain: nt

Erdős 1108 asks whether the set of finite sums of distinct factorials
contains only finitely many powerful numbers (n is powerful iff every
prime dividing n divides it with multiplicity at least 2). The full
conjecture is currently abc-conditional. We submit the bounded-support
sub-question.

theorem bounded_card_powerful_factorial_sums (K : ℕ) :
    {a : ℕ | (∃ S : Finset ℕ, S.card ≤ K ∧ a = ∑ n ∈ S, n.factorial) ∧
             (∀ p : ℕ, p.Prime → p ∣ a → p^2 ∣ a)}.Finite := by sorry

Status: OPEN unconditionally for K ≥ 2. The K=1 case is finite by
inspection. The K=2 case (sums m! + n!) has no published unconditional
proof; only abc-conditional results exist (Luca-Sárközy 1996,
Bolvardizadeh-Yavari 2025). General fixed K remains open. Slot 631
attempted the full conjecture and stalled at compiled_no_advance.
