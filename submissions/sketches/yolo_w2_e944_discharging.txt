OPEN GAP: Erdős 944 — Dirac's conjecture, k=4 case (no-critical-edge minimum-degree restriction)
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/944.lean
Domain: combinatorics

Dirac (1970) asked: for every k ≥ 4, does a finite simple graph G exist with
chromatic number k, every vertex critical (χ(G-v) < k for all v), yet every
single edge non-critical (χ(G-e) = k for all e)? The cases k ≥ 5 are
resolved positively (Brown 1992, Lattanzio 2002, Jensen 2002). The k=4 case
remains OPEN. The bare gap addressed here: any hypothetical 4-vertex-critical
graph G with no critical edge satisfies δ(G) ≥ 6 — a clean structural
restriction not previously stated for Dirac k=4.

theorem yolo_w2_erdos_944_discharging_delta_lower :
    ∀ (V : Type) [Fintype V] (G : SimpleGraph V),
      G.IsCritical 4 →
      (∀ e : Sym2 V, ¬ G.IsCriticalEdge e) →
      ∀ v : V, 6 ≤ G.degree v := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. The lemma δ(G) ≥ 6 follows from a singleton-color
pigeonhole on any 3-coloring of G - v: with d(v) ≤ 5 and all three colors
present on N(v) (forced by χ(G) = 4), some color appears exactly once on
N(v), and re-coloring v with that color gives a 3-coloring of G - uv,
contradicting no-critical-edge. Uses FormalConjecturesForMathlib's canonical
predicates SimpleGraph.IsCritical and SimpleGraph.IsCriticalEdge from
FormalConjecturesForMathlib/Combinatorics/SimpleGraph/Coloring.lean.
