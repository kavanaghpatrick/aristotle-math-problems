OPEN GAP: Erdos 850 — No three consecutive same-radical pairs
Source: FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/850.lean
Domain: nt

Prove that no distinct positive integers x, y have
rad(x)=rad(y), rad(x+1)=rad(y+1), and rad(x+2)=rad(y+2).

Computational search to 10^8 found zero k=3 witnesses.
The k=2 family x=2^n-2, y=2^n(2^n-2) cannot extend to k=3
because y+2=(2^n-1)^2+1 always has an odd prime factor.

theorem erdos_850_no_triple :
    ¬ ∃ x y : ℕ, 0 < x ∧ 0 < y ∧ x ≠ y ∧
    x.primeFactors = y.primeFactors ∧
    (x+1).primeFactors = (y+1).primeFactors ∧
    (x+2).primeFactors = (y+2).primeFactors := by sorry

Status: OPEN. No witness known to 10^8. Structural obstruction
blocks extension of all known k=2 families.
