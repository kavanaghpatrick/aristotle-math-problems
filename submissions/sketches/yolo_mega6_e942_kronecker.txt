OPEN GAP: Erdős 942 limsup — powerful counts in [n^2,(n+1)^2)
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/942.lean (variants.limsup); erdosproblems.com/942
Domain: nt

Let h(n) count powerful integers in [n^2, (n+1)^2):
  h(n) := #{m : n^2 ≤ m < (n+1)^2, ∀ prime p | m, p^2 | m}.
Erdős asserts (and erdosproblems.com confirms) limsup_{n→∞} h(n) = ∞.
This is the limsup variant; the main conjecture (existence of c > 0
with h(n) ≪ (log n)^{c+o(1)} and h(n) ≫ (log n)^{c-o(1)} i.o.)
remains open. De Koninck–Luca–Shparlinski (Bull. Austral. Math.
Soc. 71 (2005), 11–16) prove the quantitative strengthening
h(n) ≫ (log n)^{1/3 + o(1)} infinitely often.
