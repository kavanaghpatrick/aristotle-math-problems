OPEN GAP: Erdos 389 — Consecutive products divisibility
Source: FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/389.lean
Domain: nt

For every positive integer n, the product (n+1)(n+2)...(2n)
is divisible by the product 1*2*...*n. Equivalently, the
central binomial coefficient C(2n,n) is always an integer.

More generally: for every n >= 1, the product of k consecutive
integers starting from n+1 divides the product of the next k.

theorem erdos_389 : ∀ n : ℕ, n ≥ 1 →
    ∃ k : ℕ, k > 0 ∧
    (∏ i in Finset.range k, (n + 1 + i)) ∣
    (∏ i in Finset.range k, (n + k + 1 + i)) := by sorry

Status: OPEN. Known for k=n (central binomial coefficient).
