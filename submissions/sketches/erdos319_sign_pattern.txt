OPEN GAP: Erdős 319 — c(N) for sign-pattern minimal vanishing unit-fraction sums (small N)
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/319.lean
Domain: combinatorics

Let c(N) be the size of the largest A ⊆ {1,…,N} carrying signs δ : A → {-1,+1}
with Σ_{n∈A} δ(n)/n = 0 and Σ_{n∈A'} δ(n)/n ≠ 0 for every nonempty A' ⊊ A.
The general c(N) is OPEN (Croot-Adenwalla give |A| ≥ (1−1/e+o(1))N from below).
Sub-claim: establish exact c(N) for N = 6 with a witness, as an explicit lower
bound. Witness A = {1,2,3,6}, δ(1)=−1, δ(2)=δ(3)=δ(6)=+1: −1+1/2+1/3+1/6 = 0,
and every nonempty proper subset has nonzero signed sum.

theorem erdos_319_c6_lb :
    ∃ A : Finset ℕ, A ⊆ Finset.Icc 1 6 ∧ A.card = 4 ∧
      ∃ δ : ℕ → ℤˣ,
        (∑ n ∈ A, (δ n : ℚ) / n = 0) ∧
        (∀ A' ⊂ A, A'.Nonempty → ∑ n ∈ A', (δ n : ℚ) / n ≠ 0) := by sorry

Status: OPEN at the answer-of-question level. Brute-force scan gives c(N)=0
for N≤5, c(N)=4 for 6≤N≤11, c(12)=6. The bounded-existence form above is
stated as an explicit witness claim (not as `answer(sorry) ↔ …`) to avoid the
F1 undecidable-wrapper trap; the goal is exact c(N) on small N rather than
an asymptotic Θ/O/o that requires guessing the leading constant.
