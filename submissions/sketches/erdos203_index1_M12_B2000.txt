OPEN GAP: Erdos 203 — index-1 primes ≤ 2000 cannot cover the 12×12 (k,l) box
Source: FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/203.lean (slot724 follow-up)
Domain: nt

For every m ≥ 0 there is a pair (k,l) ∈ [0,12)² escaping every prime p ≤ 2000
for which the subgroup ⟨2,3⟩ equals the full group (ℤ/pℤ)* (the "index-1"
primes complementary to the index-2 set studied in slot724).

theorem index1_covering_insufficient_M12_B2000 (m : ℕ) :
    ∃ k l : ℕ, k < 12 ∧ l < 12 ∧
      ∀ p : ℕ, p.Prime → 3 < p → p ≤ 2000 →
        (((Finset.univ : Finset (Fin (p-1) × Fin (p-1))).image
          (fun ab => (2 ^ ab.1.val * 3 ^ ab.2.val) % p)).card = p - 1) →
        ¬ p ∣ 2 ^ k * 3 ^ l * m + 1 := by sorry

Status: OPEN. Slot724 proved `index2_covering_insufficient`: the 11 index-2
primes < 300 cover at most 39/100 cells in [0,10)² via `coverage_bound_for_m`
+ `native_decide` over `boxCoverage p r`. For index-1 primes the per-prime
coverage drops to 1/(p−1), but ∑1/(p−1) over such primes diverges (Dirichlet),
so this finite analog is open for every (M, B). The (M=12, B=2000) variant is
the largest of the probe triad; impossibility here would be the strongest result.
