Is there c > 0 such that the count of powerful numbers in [n^2, (n+1)^2) is ~(log n)^c?

theorem erdos_942 : answer(sorry) ↔ ∃ c > 0, ∃ (o : ℕ → ℝ), o =o[atTop] (1 : ℕ → ℝ) ∧
    (∀ᶠ n in atTop, erdos_942.h n < (Real.log n) ^ (c + o n)) ∧
    {n | erdos_942.h n > (Real.log n) ^ (c - o n)}.Infinite := by sorry

Status: OPEN. Known that limsup h(n) = infinity. The precise growth rate is unknown.
