For every odd natural number n > 1 with at most 2 distinct prime factors,
n is not multiperfect: there is no integer k ≥ 2 with σ(n) = k·n. Slot 1327
(b4069fdc, compiled clean) formalized the ω(n)=1 case (n a prime power);
the present statement extends to ω(n) ≤ 2 under an odd hypothesis. The two
smallest odd primes are 3 and 5, and (3/2)·(5/4) = 15/8 < 2 is a strict
upper bound on σ(n)/n; the bound on smaller pairs holds a fortiori. Lehmer
(1900) established that every k-perfect with k ≥ 3 has at least 3 distinct
prime factors; this special case is unformalized.
