The open BEGL96 case asks whether a set R ⊆ ℕ is cofinite. A reduction isolates the
difficulty into two local conditions at step M: if some threshold T admits a full window
of M consecutive members of R just above it, and R is closed under adding M above T, then
R contains every integer beyond T. Stated abstractly for a set R and step M ≥ 1:

Status: OPEN component (the elementary completeness engine for the open conjecture). Once
the window and +M-closure conditions hold, every n > T decomposes as n₀ + jM with n₀ in the
window, and repeated closure reaches n. The whole open difficulty of BEGL96 is establishing
the window and closure hypotheses (with M = (min D)^k); the criterion itself is elementary.
