OPEN GAP: Erdos 203 — Covering System for 2^k * 3^l * m + 1
Source: FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/203.lean
Domain: nt

Is there an integer m coprime to 6 such that 2^k * 3^l * m + 1
is never prime for any k, l >= 0?

theorem erdos_203 : answer(sorry) ↔
    ∃ m, m.Coprime 6 ∧ ∀ k l, ¬ (2^k * 3^l * m + 1).Prime := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. Related to Sierpinski/Riesel covering systems. The
analogous problem for 2^k * m + 1 (without the 3^l factor) is
solved — such odd m exist via covering congruences.
