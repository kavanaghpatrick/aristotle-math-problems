OPEN GAP: Lehmer's Totient Problem
Source: FormalConjectures/Wikipedia/LehmerTotient.lean
Domain: nt

Does there exist a composite number n > 1 such that Euler's totient
function phi(n) divides n - 1?

theorem lehmer_totient :
    answer(sorry) ↔ ∃ n > 1, ¬Prime n ∧ Nat.totient n ∣ n - 1 := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN since 1932. Any counterexample must be odd, squarefree,
have at least 14 distinct prime factors, and exceed 10^30. No
counterexample has been found.
