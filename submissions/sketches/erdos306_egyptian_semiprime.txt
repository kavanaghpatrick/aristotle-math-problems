OPEN GAP: Erdős 306 — Egyptian fractions with semiprime denominators
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/ErdosProblems/306.lean (erdos_306)
Domain: nt

Erdős–Graham (1980): every positive rational a/b with b squarefree admits an
Egyptian-fraction representation a/b = 1/n_1 + ... + 1/n_k with strictly
increasing n_i, each n_i a product of two distinct primes (ω(n_i)=Ω(n_i)=2).
Open: existence is unknown in general; the analogous three-prime version is
solved (Butler–Erdős–Graham, posthumous) for integers, but the two-prime case
remains open and is explicitly flagged as not resolvable by finite computation.

theorem erdos_306 :
    ∀ (q : ℚ), 0 < q → Squarefree q.den →
      ∃ k : ℕ, ∃ (n : Fin (k + 1) → ℕ),
        n 0 = 1 ∧ StrictMono n ∧
        (∀ i ∈ Finset.Icc 1 (Fin.last k), ω (n i) = 2 ∧ Ω (n i) = 2) ∧
        q = ∑ i ∈ Finset.Icc 1 (Fin.last k), (1 : ℚ) / (n i) := by
  sorry

Status: OPEN. Known small cases (e.g., 1/3 = 1/6 + 1/10 + 1/15) exist; the
universal statement over all positive rationals with squarefree denominator
has resisted all approaches since 1980.
