Erdos (1959): is there a polynomial f in Z[X] of degree at least 2
and a set A of integers such that for every z in Z there is EXACTLY ONE pair (a, b)
with a in A and b = f(n) for some n > 0 and z = a + b?
The case f(n) = n^2 was closed (no such A) in 1959. The cubic case f(n) = n^3 is open.
Show: no such A exists for f(n) = n^3.

theorem erdos_477_x_pow_three : letI f : ℤ[X] := X ^ 3
