Let n >= 1 and let P = product of the first (n+1) primes. Does there
always exist a prime p with p_n < p < P such that P + p is prime?

Status: FALSIFIABLE OPEN. A conjecture of Deaconescu (Math. Mag., 1982).
Verified computationally for n ≤ 1000 (Deaconescu). Erdős expected
the least such p satisfies p ≤ n^{O(1)}. Heuristic probability of
failure for any given n is ~ exp(-n^{cn}) since P = n^{(1+o(1))n}
and P+p is prime with chance ~ 1/log(P) for each candidate p.
Never previously submitted to Aristotle. Mathlib has Finset.prod,
Nat.nth, Nat.Prime, and native_decide for bounded n verification.
OEIS A005235 lists the minimal witnesses.
