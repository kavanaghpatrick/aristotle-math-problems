OPEN GAP: Brocard's Conjecture verified for n ∈ [2, 50]
Source: formal-conjectures/FormalConjectures/Wikipedia/BrocardConjecture.lean
Domain: number theory

For every n ∈ [2, 50], between the squares of the n-th and (n+1)-th primes
there are at least four prime numbers. Bounded verification — Ferreira (2023)
proved this for sufficiently large n; the gap to fully resolved is small n.

theorem brocard_conjecture_bounded :
    ∀ n : ℕ, 2 ≤ n → n ≤ 50 →
      4 ≤ ((Finset.Ioo (n.nth Nat.Prime ^ 2) ((n+1).nth Nat.Prime ^ 2)).filter Nat.Prime).card := by sorry

Status: OPEN. Brocard's conjecture is unresolved in general. This bounded
version reduces to a finite check; native_decide is the natural tactic.
