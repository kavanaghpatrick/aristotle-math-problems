For any positive integers n and d with gcd(n, d) = 1, the product
n(n+d)(n+2d)(n+3d) is never a perfect cube. Equivalently: there exists
N0 such that for all n >= N0 and all positive d coprime to n, no m in N
satisfies n(n+d)(n+2d)(n+3d) = m^3.
