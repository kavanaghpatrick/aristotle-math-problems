OPEN GAP: Erdős Problem 647 — Divisor Function Gaps
Source: Erdős Problem 647
Domain: nt

Is there n > 24 such that max_{m < n} (m + τ(m)) ≤ n + 2,
where τ(m) is the number of divisors of m?

For n ≤ 24, the maximum of m + τ(m) over m < n can be ≤ n + 2.
The question is whether this "gap" phenomenon persists for large n.

Since τ(m) ≥ 2 for m ≥ 2, we always have m + τ(m) ≥ m + 2.
The density of highly composite numbers and the average
τ(m) ~ log m suggest gaps should become rarer.
