# Tuza's Conjecture - Complete the Inductive Proof

## Goal
Prove Tuza's conjecture: τ(G) ≤ 2ν(G) where:
- τ(G) = minimum number of edges to delete to make G triangle-free (triangle covering number)
- ν(G) = maximum number of edge-disjoint triangles (triangle packing number)

## What Remains

### The Key Inductive Step Lemma
You need to prove:
```lean
lemma tuza_inductive_step
  {V : Type} [Fintype V] [DecidableEq V] (G : SimpleGraph V) [DecidableRel G.Adj]
  (h : trianglePackingNumber G > 0) :
  ∃ S : Finset (Sym2 V), S ⊆ G.edgeFinset ∧ S.card ≤ 2 ∧
    trianglePackingNumber (G.deleteEdges S) < trianglePackingNumber G
```

### Strategy for the Inductive Step
The key insight is: Given a maximum triangle packing, pick any triangle T in the packing.
- If we delete 2 edges of T, we definitely reduce ν by at least 1
- The question is: do we reduce it by exactly 1, or could we increase it elsewhere?

**Approach**:
1. Take any triangle T in a maximum packing
2. Delete two edges of T (say edges e1, e2)
3. Show that ν(G - {e1, e2}) ≤ ν(G) - 1

This follows because:
- T cannot be in any packing of G - {e1, e2} (missing edges)
- Any new triangle packing in G - {e1, e2} was also a valid packing in G
- But it couldn't be larger than the maximum, so ν decreases by at least 1
