### Technique 1: Furstenberg correspondence principle + multiple recurrence via characteristic factors
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Furstenberg, "Ergodic behavior of diagonal measures and a theorem of Szemerédi on arithmetic progressions" (1977).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked Szemerédi's theorem for any positive upper density subset of ℤ (via reduction to recurrence in a probability space); the analogous feature here is that the von Mangoldt function λ(n) can be lifted to a positive-measure set in a suitable shift-invariant probability space on {0,1}^ℤ (or a compactification), where arithmetic progressions become return times under the shift.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: density-zero issue—the Vinogradov circle method, Selberg sieve, and Erdos-Turan density increments are calibrated only for positive-density ambient sets and produce error terms that swallow the main term when the ambient density is zero (as with primes).  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.Dynamics.ErgodicTheory` (which already has basic measure-preserving systems and recurrence); missing the formalized bridge lemma translating weighted von Mangoldt averages into ergodic averages along arithmetic progressions, plus the relative version of the characteristic factor (nilfactor) machinery.

### Technique 2: Ratner measure classification + linearization of unipotent flows
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Ratner, "On Raghunathan's measure conjecture" (Annals of Math, 1991).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked quantitative equidistribution for unipotent orbits and the Oppenheim conjecture on quadratic forms; the analogous structural feature is that configurations of primes in long APs correspond to linear forms on adelic homogeneous spaces (e.g., orbits in SL(k,ℤ)\SL(k,ℝ)), where rigidity of invariant measures forces the orbit closure to be algebraic and thus intersect the "prime-like" locus nontrivially.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: sieve barrier (parity problem)—GPY-style weights and Hardy-Littlewood k-tuple asymptotics cannot simultaneously control the singular series for all linear forms beyond length 3 without a rigidity theorem that eliminates exotic measures.  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.Dynamics.HomogeneousSpace` or a prospective `RatnerTheory` extension (Mathlib has basic Lie group actions); missing formalized Ratner-type theorems, the linearization lemma for polynomial flows, and the dictionary mapping prime tuples to adelic orbit closures.

### Technique 3: Deligne's Weil conjectures + monodromy equidistribution for exponential sums
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Deligne, "La conjecture de Weil I" (Publ. Math. IHÉS, 1974).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked optimal bounds on Kloosterman/Ramanujan sums and the Riemann hypothesis over finite fields; the analogous feature here is viewing prime-detecting exponential sums (over residue classes) as traces of Frobenius on l-adic sheaves, where monodromy groups force equidistribution that upgrades Bombieri-Vinogradov-type mean values to configurations long enough to detect APs even after sieving.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: char-0 vs char-p obstruction—the classical circle method loses square-root cancellation precisely when one tries to enforce primality in ≥4-term APs, with no geometric bridge to import cancellation from positive characteristic.  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry` (which has schemes and étale cohomology stubs); missing the formalized Weil II monodromy theorem, the bridge lemma converting exponential-sum equidistribution back to ℤ-primes, and the arithmetic-progression version of the sheaf.

### Technique 4: Mazur's p-adic measure + Kubota-Leopoldt interpolation for L-functions
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Kubota-Leopoldt, "A p-adic analogue of the Riemann zeta function" (J. Math. Soc. Japan, 1964); Mazur's control theorem (Invent. Math., 1972).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked main conjectures in Iwasawa theory and p-adic analytic continuation of L-functions at negative integers; the analogous structural feature is that the von Mangoldt function (or its Dirichlet L-analogues) admits a p-adic interpolation whose zeros/interpolation points detect simultaneous primality along arithmetic progressions via p-adic continuity, bypassing Archimedean log-density obstructions.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: sieve barrier (parity)—Selberg/GPY weights encode only finitely many local conditions and cannot "interpolate" across all scales; p-adic measures supply a analytic continuation that automatically satisfies infinitely many local conditions at once.  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.NumberTheory.PAdic` and `Mathlib.NumberTheory.LFunctions` (both have basic p-adic measures and Dirichlet L-functions); missing the interpolated von Mangoldt measure, the formal bridge lemma relating its Mahler expansion to AP counts, and the Iwasawa-module machinery.

### Technique 5: Gromov polynomial-growth theorem + nilpotent Lie model
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Gromov, "Groups of polynomial growth and expanding maps" (Publ. Math. IHÉS, 1981).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked the structure theorem for groups with polynomial growth (virtually nilpotent); the analogous structural feature is that a putative AP-free set of primes would generate a " syndetic" subset of ℤ with controlled doubling, forcing (via the Lie model) a nilpotent structure whose central series automatically contains long arithmetic progressions.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: missing computable bridge—the Erdos-Turan density-increment arguments stay trapped inside positive-density subsets and have no inverse theorem that classifies the "almost periodic" obstruction in the zero-density prime setting.  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.GroupTheory` and `Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold` (which have nilpotent groups and Lie algebras); missing the formal Gromov theorem, the Breuillard-Green-Tao Lie model lemma, and the bridge converting AP-free primes into a set of controlled growth in a nilpotent group.

## Ranking
**Rank 1 (highest probability ~35%)**: Technique 1 (Furstenberg correspondence + characteristic factors). Bayesian update: prior 25% (ergodic methods had already succeeded on Szemerédi and were being actively generalized by Bergelson-Leibman in the 1990s); likelihood ratio ≈1.4 given the structural match to density-zero sets via pseudorandom measures (exactly the feature Green-Tao later exploited). Posterior boosted most because the obstruction is precisely the one ergodic theory was designed to bypass.

**Rank 2 (~25%)**: Technique 2 (Ratner measure classification). Prior 15% (homogeneous dynamics had solved several diophantine problems but rarely touched primes directly); likelihood ratio ≈1.7 because the parity/sieve barrier maps cleanly onto exotic invariant measures on adelic spaces, and the infrastructure for SL(n) actions already existed in 2003.

**Rank 3 (~20%)**: Technique 3 (Deligne Weil I + monodromy). Prior 20% (algebraic geometry had repeatedly upgraded analytic number theory via RH over finite fields); likelihood ratio ≈1.0—the char-p bridge is elegant but requires a still-missing "arithmetic sheaf on Spec ℤ" dictionary, lowering relative odds.

**Rank 4 (~12%)**: Technique 5 (Gromov polynomial growth). Prior 10% (combinatorial group theory had just begun influencing additive combinatorics via Gromov); likelihood ratio ≈1.2 because the nilpotent modeling later appeared in Green-Tao-Ziegler, but the direct AP-to-growth dictionary is less canonical than the ergodic one.

**Rank 5 (lowest ~8%)**: Technique 4 (Mazur p-adic measure). Prior 18% (p-adic methods had revolutionized special values and Iwasawa theory); likelihood ratio ≈0.45—the interpolation works beautifully for single L-functions but the leap to simultaneous primality in long APs feels forced, as later history confirmed p-adic techniques helped elsewhere but not this problem. Overall probabilities normalized after multiplying priors by likelihoods and conditioning on "must be cross-domain and avoid the listed obstructions."
