### Technique 1: Ratner's orbit-closure theorem + Dani-Margulis linearization
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Ratner, "On Raghunathan's measure conjecture" (Annals of Mathematics, 1991).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked Oppenheim conjecture on values of indefinite quadratic forms (Margulis 1987–89); analogous feature is the Jacobian embedding of C into A_g (moduli of principally polarized abelian varieties), where isogeny classes correspond to orbits under unipotent flows on the homogeneous space Sp(2g,ℤ)\Sp(2g,ℝ), with height boundedness translating to non-divergence of the orbit.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: density-zero issue (Mordell–Weil + Neron–Tate heights produce only density-zero control on isogeny classes inside A_g, with no mechanism to force orbit closures to be algebraic).  
**Implementation comment**: Carried in `dynamics.homogeneous` or `measure_theory.ergodic` namespace; missing is formalization of Ratner measure classification, the period map from Jacobians to the homogeneous space, and the bridge lemma translating bounded Faltings height to non-divergence.

### Technique 2: Pila–Wilkie o-minimal point-counting + definable Lindemann–Weierstrass
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Pila–Wilkie, "The rational points of a definable set" (Duke Mathematical Journal, 2006).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked Manin–Mumford conjecture (Pila–Zannier 2008); analogous feature is the uniformization of Jac(C) by its period domain, viewed as a definable set in an o-minimal expansion of ℝ (e.g., via restricted Pfaffian functions), so that C(K) lifts to special points whose counting function is controlled by the o-minimal dimension.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: missing computable bridge (Thue–Siegel–Roth and Chabauty give no effective transcendental uniformization that separates algebraic points from the ambient analytic variety without o-minimal tameness).  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in a new `o_minimal` namespace (extending `model_theory.definable`); Mathlib entirely lacks o-minimal structures, Wilkie’s theorem, and the definability proof for the Betti map of a curve of genus ≥2.

### Technique 3: Grothendieck anabelian reconstruction + section conjecture for hyperbolic curves
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Grothendieck, "Esquisse d’un programme" (1984 manuscript; circulated letter to Faltings 1983).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked anabelian reconstruction of number fields and once-punctured elliptic curves (Mochizuki’s later work on mono-anabelian geometry); analogous feature is that C(K) bijects with Gal(K̄/K)-sections of the exact sequence 1 → π₁^ét(C̄) → π₁^ét(C) → Gal(K̄/K) → 1, and the section conjecture implies only finitely many such sections can be “integral” with respect to the compactification.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: missing computable bridge (classical heights and reduction modulo primes detect only abelianized Galois action on Tate modules; they give no handle on the profinite combinatorial group structure of the étale fundamental group).  
**Implementation comment**: Would sit in `algebraic_geometry.etale_fundamental` or `number_theory.anabelian`; Mathlib has basic étale cohomology but lacks formalization of profinite groupoids, outer Galois actions on π₁, and the reconstruction functor from Galois categories.

### Technique 4: p-adic Hodge theory + Fontaine–Mazur crystalline comparison
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Fontaine, “Le corps des périodes p-adiques” (Astérisque, 1982) together with the crystalline comparison theorem (later completed by Fontaine–Messing 1987).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked proof of the Mordell–Weil theorem over function fields in char p (via crystalline cohomology); analogous feature here is that the p-adic Galois representation on T_ℓ(Jac(C)) must be crystalline at primes of good reduction, forcing the Hodge filtration to arise from a unique filtered φ-module whose Dieudonné module classifies only finitely many isogeny classes of bounded conductor.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: char-0 vs char-p (classical Chabauty–Coleman works only when the Mordell–Weil rank is strictly less than the genus; it supplies no comparison isomorphism that upgrades ℓ-adic images to crystalline data usable for all primes simultaneously).  
**Implementation comment**: Belongs in `p_adic_hodge_theory` (extending existing `padic` and `galois_representation` namespaces); Mathlib has basic p-adic fields but lacks Fontaine rings B_dR/B_crys, the comparison functors, and the bridge lemma translating bounded conductor to finite filtered φ-modules.

### Technique 5: Simpson correspondence for Higgs bundles + non-abelian Hodge harmonic metric
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Simpson, “Higgs bundles and local systems” (Publications Mathématiques de l’IHÉS, 1992).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked Shafarevich-type finiteness for representations of π₁ of varieties of general type (Simpson 1990–92); analogous feature is that the Higgs bundle (E,θ) associated to the universal family over the moduli space of curves carries a harmonic Hermitian metric whose energy functional gives an Arakelov-type height bound, forcing only finitely many isomorphism classes inside a given isogeny class.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: height bound (Neron–Tate and Mordell–Weil give only quadratic growth; they cannot produce the differential-geometric stability inequality needed to compactify the moduli stack without the harmonic-map input).  
**Implementation comment**: Would require a new `algebraic_geometry.higgs` or `differential_geometry.harmonic_maps` namespace; Mathlib has almost nothing on Higgs bundles, stability conditions, or the non-abelian Hodge correspondence.

## Ranking
**Rank 1 (≈22% posterior probability)**: Grothendieck anabelian reconstruction + section conjecture. Bayesian update: prior ≈35% (anabelian methods already resolved analogous finiteness statements for hyperbolic curves over finite fields and some number-field cases by 2010s); likelihood ratio ≈0.63 given this problem’s explicit reduction to isogeny classes of Jacobians (whose Galois action on π₁ is precisely what the section conjecture controls). Highest because the bridge is almost tautological once the profinite group is formalized.

**Rank 2 (≈18%)**: p-adic Hodge theory + Fontaine–Mazur crystalline comparison. Prior ≈30% (p-adic Hodge has succeeded on every finiteness problem where a crystalline lift exists, e.g., Fontaine–Mazur for Galois representations); likelihood ≈0.6 because Parshin’s reduction is exactly about good reduction outside a fixed set S, which crystalline cohomology directly bounds. Only slightly below anabelian because it still needs a global argument to glue local crystalline data.

**Rank 3 (≈12%)**: Simpson correspondence for Higgs bundles + non-abelian Hodge. Prior ≈25% (non-abelian Hodge directly implies Shafarevich-type theorems in higher dimension); likelihood ≈0.48 given that the moduli space of curves of genus ≥2 is already known to be of log-general type—the harmonic metric supplies exactly the missing positivity that classical Neron–Tate heights cannot see. Downgraded because it is differential-geometric and may require extra compactification steps not native to the curve.

**Rank 4 (≈9%)**: Ratner’s orbit-closure + Dani–Margulis linearization. Prior ≈40% (homogeneous dynamics has near-100% success rate on Diophantine problems once an orbit can be attached); likelihood only ≈0.22 here because constructing a natural unipotent flow whose closed orbits correspond exactly to isogeny classes of Jacobians of fixed genus is still conjectural (requires a yet-unformalized “arithmetic Ratner” for A_g). Strong prior but structural mismatch lowers posterior.

**Rank 5 (≈6%)**: Pila–Wilkie o-minimal point-counting. Prior ≈20% (o-minimality gives uniform bounds wherever period maps are definable); likelihood ≈0.3 because definability of the Betti map for genus ≥2 curves over number fields remains open (current proofs stop at abelian varieties of dimension 1 or Shimura varieties of low dimension). Lowest because the requisite o-minimal structure is not known to exist for the Jacobian period map of a general curve of genus ≥2.
