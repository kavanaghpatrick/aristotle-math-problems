### Technique 1: slice rank method (Tao symmetrization bridge lemma)
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Tao, “A symmetric formulation of the Croot–Lev–Pach–Ellenberg–Gijswijt capset bound”, blog post + arXiv:1605.06774 (2016).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked the capset bound in \(\mathbb{Z}_4^n\) (Croot–Lev–Pach 2016) by converting a combinatorial forbidden configuration into a low-rank symmetric 3-tensor; the precise analog here is that a cap set \(A\subset\mathbb{F}_3^n\) forces the indicator tensor \(1_A(x)1_A(y)1_A(z)\) supported on \(x+y+z=0\) to be supported only on the diagonal \(x=y=z\), exactly the setting where slice-rank decomposition yields a dimension-counting exponential upper bound.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: density-zero issue—the Roth-style density-increment iteration only extracts a \(1/n\) saving before the density drops below the Fourier bias threshold, with no global control on the underlying tensor; Fourier methods cannot “see” the global linear-algebra rank of the full 3-tensor.  
**Implementation comment**: Lives in `Mathlib.LinearAlgebra.TensorProduct` or a prospective `Mathlib.Combinatorics.Additive.CapSet`; missing are the definitions of slice rank for 3-tensors, the symmetrization lemma, and the explicit rank bound for the \(\mathbb{F}_3\)-Cayley tensor \(x+y+z=0\).

### Technique 2: Host–Kra structure theorem (characteristic nilfactor lemma)
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Host–Kra, “Nonconventional ergodic averages and nilmanifolds”, Ann. of Math. (2) 161 (2005).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked convergence of multiple ergodic averages and quantitative Szemerédi theorems in \(\mathbb{Z}\)-actions; the analogous feature here is that \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\) with translation action is a compact abelian group system, a cap set is a set with no return times under the 3-term relation \((x,x+d,x+2d)\), and the characteristic factor is a nilmanifold whose dimension would directly bound the cap-set size.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: missing computable bridge—the Meshulam Fourier argument only controls the linear (U^1) eigenfunctions; the Host–Kra machinery identifies the precise higher-step nilfactor obstruction that classical Fourier analysis cannot isolate in characteristic 3 without an inverse theorem.  
**Implementation comment**: Would sit in `Mathlib.Dynamics.ErgodicTheory` (which already contains basic measure-preserving systems); missing are the Host–Kra structure theorem for \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\)-actions, the explicit nilfactor for the 3-term progression, and a quantitative equidistribution lemma translating nilmanifold dimension back to a cardinality bound.

### Technique 3: Deligne’s Weil II (étale cohomology purity lemma)
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Deligne, “La conjecture de Weil. II”, Publ. Math. IHÉS 52 (1980).  
**Structural analog**: Gave sharp Weil-type bounds on exponential sums over varieties, unlocking the Bombieri–Vinogradov theorem in function fields and sharp character-sum estimates; the structural analog is that the Fourier transform of a cap-set indicator is a sum over the hypersurface \(x+y+z=0\) in \(\mathbb{F}_3^{3n}\), so purity of weights on the associated l-adic sheaf yields square-root cancellation stronger than any classical Fourier bound.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: char-0 vs char-p—the Meshulam Fourier analysis works entirely inside characteristic 3 and cannot lift to characteristic 0 to exploit Weil conjectures; the Riemann-hypothesis-over-finite-fields cancellation is invisible to Bohr-set or density-increment methods.  
**Implementation comment**: Would require `Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry.EtaleCohomology` (currently only skeletal in mathlib); missing are the formalism of l-adic sheaves on \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\)-schemes, the purity lemma, and the bridge converting a cap-set indicator into an étale sheaf whose weights give the desired exponential decay.

### Technique 4: Ratner’s measure-classification theorem (orbit-closure rigidity lemma)
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Ratner, “On Raghunathan’s measure conjecture”, Ann. of Math. (2) 134 (1991).  
**Structural analog**: Solved the Oppenheim conjecture on values of quadratic forms by classifying invariant measures on homogeneous spaces; the analog is to embed a putative large cap set into an adelic homogeneous space \(\mathrm{SL}_n(\mathbb{Q})\backslash\mathrm{SL}_n(\mathbb{A})\) where the 3-AP condition becomes a unipotent orbit closure, whose algebraic structure (by Ratner) forces the set to lie in a proper subvariety, yielding an exponential volume bound.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: height bound—the iterative density-increment produces sets that are “flat” in Fourier space but whose adelic height grows too fast for classical harmonic analysis to control; Ratner rigidity classifies all possible limiting measures at infinite height.  
**Implementation comment**: Would live in `Mathlib.Dynamics.HomogeneousSpace` or `Mathlib.LieTheory`; missing are formalizations of Ratner’s theorems, the specific unipotent flow corresponding to 3-APs in \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\), and the height-to-volume dictionary that turns rigidity into a cap-set cardinality bound.

### Technique 5: combinatorial Nullstellensatz (Alon’s polynomial-permutation bridge)
**Seminal paper or theorem**: Alon, “Combinatorial Nullstellensatz”, Combin. Probab. Comput. 8 (1999).  
**Structural analog**: Unlocked tight bounds for permanent-free matrices and rainbow matchings; the analogous feature is that a large cap set in \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\) can be viewed as a zero set of a multivariate polynomial whose degree is controlled by the 3-AP condition, so the Nullstellensatz plus a permutation polynomial argument produces a degree-versus-size contradiction giving an exponential upper bound.  
**Diagnosis of why the default toolkit failed**: sieve barrier—Fourier/Roth methods rely on additive energy increments that cannot cross the “singular” regime where the set is algebraically dependent; the Nullstellensatz directly detects algebraic dependence without sieving.  
**Implementation comment**: Exists in rudimentary form in `Mathlib.Algebra.Polynomial`; missing are the multivariate Nullstellensatz over finite fields, the explicit permutation-polynomial construction for the cap-set equation, and the Lean tactic infrastructure to automate degree counting on \(\mathbb{F}_3^n\).

## Ranking
**Rank 1: slice rank method (Tao symmetrization bridge lemma)** — Prior ≈ 0.35 (it had already succeeded for the \(\mathbb{Z}_4^n\) variant months earlier; Bayesian update multiplies by likelihood ≈ 0.9 given the identical 3-linear tensor structure in char 3) → posterior ≈ 0.32.  
**Rank 2: combinatorial Nullstellensatz (Alon’s polynomial-permutation bridge)** — Prior ≈ 0.20 (worked on many configuration-avoidance problems in extremal set theory); likelihood ≈ 0.6 because the cap-set condition is precisely a polynomial vanishing statement, but the precise degree control is less automatic than slice rank → posterior ≈ 0.12.  
**Rank 3: Host–Kra structure theorem (characteristic nilfactor lemma)** — Prior ≈ 0.15 (high success rate on Szemerédi-type problems via ergodic theory); likelihood ≈ 0.4 because quantitative nilmanifold bounds in char-3 vector spaces are notoriously hard to extract, lowering the posterior to ≈ 0.06.  
**Rank 4: Deligne’s Weil II (étale cohomology purity lemma)** — Prior ≈ 0.10 (transformed analytic number theory over finite fields); likelihood ≈ 0.3—the Fourier transform of a cap set does sit on a variety, but converting purity directly into a cap-set cardinality bound requires several non-obvious sheaf constructions, posterior ≈ 0.03.  
**Rank 5: Ratner’s measure-classification theorem (orbit-closure rigidity lemma)** — Prior ≈ 0.05 (spectacular on homogeneous dynamics but rarely applied to pure combinatorial questions); likelihood ≈ 0.2 because the adelic embedding of a cap set is artificial and the height control seems loose, posterior ≈ 0.01.  

Cumulative posterior mass on these five ideas is >0.5; the remainder is spread over dozens of other exotic techniques, but the slice-rank approach dominates because its structural match is nearly perfect.
