The analogous structural feature is encoding primality in 𝔽_p by the polynomial (x^{p-1} − 1) · ((p−1)! + 1 ≡ 0 mod p via Wilson); the hypersurface x + y + z − n = 0 then has a non-vanishing leading coefficient in the product ideal, guaranteeing a solution triple for every odd n mod p, which lifts by Chinese remainder theorem to all n ≥ 7.

## Ranking
**Rank 1: Technique 1 (Gowers inverse theorem)**. Prior ≈ 0.45 (Green-Tao already succeeded on linear configurations in primes with exactly this tool, high success rate in adjacent additive problems); likelihood ≈ 0.8 given this problem's exact 3-linear form p+q+r=n matches the inverse theorem's sweet spot for s=2; posterior ≈ 0.36. Highest because it directly transplants the machinery that already handles primes in linear equations while bypassing L-functions.

**Rank 2: Technique 2 (Host-Kra structure theorem)**. Prior ≈ 0.35 (ergodic theory routinely transfers Szemerédi to arithmetic settings via correspondence); likelihood ≈ 0.65 (multiple recurrence perfectly matches the 3-fold average over primes, but requires extra work to handle the von Mangoldt measure); posterior ≈ 0.23. Strong but slightly lower than Gowers because the correspondence is less explicit for effective constants on every single n.

**Rank 3: Technique 3 (Ratner's theorem)**. Prior ≈ 0.25 (dynamics on homogeneous spaces solved Oppenheim and gave effective equidistribution in other Diophantine problems); likelihood ≈ 0.55 (requires a non-obvious adelic embedding of the Goldbach equation, but orbit closures would uniformly rule out exceptions); posterior ≈ 0.14. Plausible cross-domain bridge but embedding step lowers likelihood.

**Rank 4: Technique 4 (Deligne monodromy)**. Prior ≈ 0.20 (alg-geo equidistribution gave power savings in many exponential-sum problems but rarely replaces entire analytic NT arguments); likelihood ≈ 0.40 (sheaf-theoretic model of minor arcs works but still indirectly improves rather than bypasses the circle method); posterior ≈ 0.08. Useful for effective constants yet farther from a fully new proof structure.

**Rank 5: Technique 5 (combinatorial nullstellensatz)**. Prior ≈ 0.10 (polynomial method succeeds in finite-field additive combinatorics but has never scaled to infinite primes); likelihood ≈ 0.25 (Wilson polynomial encoding of primality is awkward in characteristic 0 and produces only existential, not quantitative, results for all n); posterior ≈ 0.025. Lowest because the lift from mod p to ℤ is non-obvious and the method has weakest track record on prime-detecting polynomials. Overall Bayesian update favors additive-combinatorial tools most heavily given the linear-equation structure of ternary Goldbach.
