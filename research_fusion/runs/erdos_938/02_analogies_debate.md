## Top pick rationale
The highest-confidence analogy is **Dejean's Conjecture** within the domain of **combinatorics on words**. This problem is structurally unique because it demands the 3-term arithmetic progression be formed by *consecutive* terms ($n_k, n_{k+1}, n_{k+2}$). This constraint transforms the number-theoretic problem into a pattern-avoidance problem on the sequence of first differences $d_k = n_{k+1} - n_k$; specifically, the existence of such an AP is equivalent to the appearance of a 'square' (a repeated block $d_k, d_k$) in the gap sequence. The **Currie-Rampersad Template Method** is the seminal technique for proving the finiteness or existence of such repetitions in infinite words. While number theory provides the "alphabet" (the set of possible gaps), the core obstruction is the "consecutive" combinatorial structure, making word combinatorics the most faithful mapping. This perspective shifts the focus from the density of powerful numbers to the local symbolic dynamics of their growth.
