```text
d <= sqrt(n_{k+2}) + 1,
```

or in the looser slot-1315 form,

```text
d < 2 sqrt(N) + 2.
```

Chan’s abc-conditional lower bound gives, for every `eps > 0`,

```text
d >>_eps N^{1/2 - eps}.
```

See Chan arXiv:2210.00281, abstract: https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.00281.

Thus, under abc, a consecutive powerful 3-AP has gap essentially of square-root size:

```text
N^{1/2-o(1)} <= d <= N^{1/2+o(1)}.
```

This does not prove finiteness. It only says the gap has the same order as the unavoidable square gaps.

Combined with the bounded-kernel finiteness above, any infinite family must enter the unbounded-kernel regime. Since

```text
n_i = a_i^2 b_i^3 ~ N,
```

large `b_i` forces

```text
a_i ~ N^{1/2} / b_i^{3/2}.
```

So kernel growth compresses the square part. The AP identity

```text
(n_{k+1})^2 = n_k n_{k+2} + d^2
```

becomes

```text
a_2^4 b_2^6 - a_1^2 a_3^2 (b_1 b_3)^3 = d^2.
```

Together with `d ~ sqrt(N)` under abc, this gives a genuine diophantine constraint coupling kernel growth and square-part shrinkage. This is the right place for a “kernel-uniformity” lemma.

4. **Proposed Kernel-Uniformity Lemma** — **OPEN / SPECULATIVE**

A useful load-bearing lemma would be:

> For every infinite family of consecutive powerful 3-APs satisfying the abc gap window, the squarefree kernels `(b_1,b_2,b_3)` cannot grow without producing, after a controlled normalization, a bounded-length coprime powerful AP structure forbidden by Bajpai-Bennett-Chan under abc.

More concretely, one would need to show that the equation

```text
a_2^4 b_2^6 - a_1^2 a_3^2 (b_1 b_3)^3 = d^2,
```

plus

```text
d = a_2^2 b_2^3 - a_1^2 b_1^3
  = a_3^2 b_3^3 - a_2^2 b_2^3
```

forces a uniform bound on the squarefree kernels unless one can extract a forbidden coprime powerful 5-AP.

This is not currently proved by the stated inputs. It is the missing bridge.

5. **BBC Coprime Result Does Not Directly Apply** — **BBC-CONDITIONAL but insufficient**

Bajpai-Bennett-Chan prove, unconditionally, infinitely many 4-term APs of pairwise-coprime powerful numbers, and under abc their Corollary 1.3 gives

```text
A_infty(2) = 4,
```

i.e. no 5-term pairwise-coprime powerful APs under abc. See arXiv:2302.03113: https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.03113.

But a consecutive powerful 3-AP need not be coprime. Dividing by the gcd does not preserve powerfulness. For example:

```text
1728, 1764, 1800
gcd = 36
48, 49, 50
```

The reduced triple is coprime as a triple, but it is not a triple of powerful numbers.

So BBC does not presently give a kernel bound for consecutive powerful 3-APs. To use BBC, one must prove an additional normalization theorem:

> Every sufficiently large unbounded-kernel consecutive powerful 3-AP canonically yields a pairwise-coprime powerful AP of length at least 5.

That theorem is OPEN. Without it, BBC’s `A_infty(2)=4` forbids the wrong object.

6. **Final Status** — **OPEN**

The synthesis gives a clean conditional strategy, but it does not close E938.

What is proved/usable:

```text
bounded kernels -> finite, unconditionally per kernel triple;
infinite family -> kernels unbounded;
abc -> d has square-root size;
unbounded kernels -> strong diophantine pressure.
```

What remains open:

```text
unbounded kernels -> BBC-forbidden coprime powerful 5-AP
```

or any equivalent uniform kernel bound.

So the proposed load-bearing claim is exactly the kernel-uniformity lemma above. It is plausible as a research target, but it is not a consequence of BBC as currently stated.
