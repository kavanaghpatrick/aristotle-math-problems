# E938 deep-Hooley dossier — Literature (May 30 2026)

## Primary citations
- Hooley, C. "An asymptotic formula in the theory of numbers." Proc. London Math. Soc. (3) 7 (1957), 396-412. Original dispersion-style argument for additive sums.
- Hooley, C. *Applications of Sieve Methods to the Theory of Numbers*, Cambridge Tracts in Mathematics 70, Cambridge University Press 1976. ISBN 0-521-20915-3, 122pp.
- Hooley, C. "An application of the Selberg sieve to a problem of Erdős and Mahler." Acta Arithmetica 33 (1979), 117-127. (Codex Round 2 alternative — Selberg sieve on representation multiplicities.)
- Chan, T.H. "Arithmetic progressions among powerful numbers." arXiv:2210.00281 (2022). d ≫_ε N^{1/2-ε} under abc.
- van Doorn, W. "Three-term arithmetic progressions of consecutive powerful numbers." arXiv:2605.06697 (May 2026). Pellian construction at d=2√N+1; 18 consecutive triples found up to 10^14, all from set A_1 (single-square); CONJECTURES infinitely many consecutive, would falsify E938.
- Bajpai, Bennett, Chan. "Arithmetic Progressions in Squarefull Numbers." arXiv:2302.03113. Conditional results on k-full APs under abc.
- Heath-Brown, D.R. "Square-full numbers in short intervals." Math. Proc. Cambridge Philos. Soc. (1991/1992). Anatomy reduction Q(x) → S(x) = #{a²b³ ≤ x}.
- Nunes, R. "Squarefree numbers in arithmetic progressions." arXiv:1402.0684 (2014) and arXiv:1602.00311 (2016). Dispersion for squarefree, NOT powerful.
- Mangerel, A. "Squarefree Integers in Arithmetic Progressions to Smooth Moduli." arXiv:2008.11163. Modern dispersion extension.

## Negative literature finding
Grok Round 1 (web-searched): **No published application of Hooley dispersion to the powerful indicator exists**. The dimension-1/2 sieve has been treated for squarefree, almost-prime, divisor functions, k-free, but never for powerful numbers / squareful numbers. This is virgin territory.
