### 1. Frey-Hellegouarch curve + Ribet level-lowering

*   **Structural Analog**: The resolution of $x^n + y^n = 2z^2$. E938 is a ternary equation $n_1 + n_3 = 2n_2$. By writing powerful numbers in their $a^2b^3$ form, we can attach a Frey curve whose discriminant is composed solely of the primes dividing the triple.
*   **Diagnosis of Bare-Gap Failure**: Bare-gap solvers only check if a solution exists for $n < N$ using local residues. They cannot "see" the Galois representation of the associated elliptic curve. *   **Mathlib Feasibility**: Requires `AlgebraicGeometry.EllipticCurve` (exists) and `RepresentationTheory.Galois` (exists). **Missing**: The link between `CuspForm` and `GaloisRepresentation` (Ribet's Theorem), which is the "computable bridge" we lack.
