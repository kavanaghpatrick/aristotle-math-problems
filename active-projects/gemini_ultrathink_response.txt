### 1. Ramsey Number R(4,6) Lower Bound

**Statement**: Prove that the Ramsey number $R(4,6) > 36$. (Find a coloring of $K_{36}$ with no clique of size 4 and no independent set of size 6).

**Domain**: Extremal Combinatorics / Ramsey Theory

**Open Since**: 1970s (Lower bound 35 stood for decades, improved to 36 in 2012).

**Why Groundbreaking**: Ramsey numbers are the "drosophila" of combinatorics. Moving a bound for a classical case like $R(4,6)$ is a major result.

**Why Tractable**: $R(4,6)$ is known to be $\le 41$. The search space is graphs on $36-40$ vertices. This is vast ($2^{700}$), but heavily structured. Aristotle can exploit symmetry breaking and SAT clause learning to find a counter-example (a coloring) if one exists, or provide a certificate of unsatisfiability (proving $R(4,6) \le 36$).

**Search Space Estimate**: $2^{\binom{36}{2}} \approx 2^{630}$ (Reduced by symmetry to $\sim 2^{60}$ effective with smart breaking).

**Recent Progress**: 2024 upper bound improvements for $R(5,5)$ and general $R(s,t)$ by geometric methods suggest renewed interest and better understanding of the structural limits.

**Scores**:
- Groundbreaking: 8/10

### 2. Kissing Number in Dimension 5

**Statement**: Does there exist a configuration of 41 unit spheres in $\mathbb{R}^5$ such that all touch a central unit sphere? (i.e., exists a set of 41 vectors in $S^4$ with pairwise dot products $\le 0.5$).

**Domain**: Discrete Geometry

**Open Since**: 1950s (Bounds $40 \le k(5) \le 44$).

**Why Groundbreaking**: The kissing number problem is solved only for dimensions 1, 2, 3, 4, 8, and 24. Dimension 5 is the *smallest open case*. Solving it would be historic.

**Why Tractable**: This can be relaxed to a graph problem or Semidefinite Programming (SDP) problem. Aristotle can search for a graph structure compatible with the geometric constraints (using "forbidden subgraph" logic) or find a specific coordinate construction.

**Search Space Estimate**: Continuous, but discretizable. Structure space is effectively finite (graphs on 41 vertices).

**Recent Progress**: 2023 construction saturated the lower bound of 40. New methods in SDP (2024) have tightened upper bounds for other dimensions.

**Scores**:
- Groundbreaking: 10/10
- Tractability: 6/10 (Geometric constraints are harder for SAT)
- Formalizability: 9/10
- Priority: **5.40**

**Boris Submission Strategy**: "Lean 4 definition: A set of 41 points in 5D space with pairwise distance >= 1. Verify consistency."

**Recent Progress**: 2014-2024 improvement in SAT solvers for discrepancy problems.
